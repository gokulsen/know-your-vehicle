How to Identify Fuel Points

Image: Fuel Gauge - Analog & Digital
This shows a typical fuel gauge - Count the number of segments or points from empty (E) to full (F). For example, if there are 10 segments, the "Full Points" would be 10. When the gauge is at half capacity, then points would be 5. Use this to estimate the "Remaining Points" after a trip.

Calculation

To determine the mileage of your vehicle, first, you need to calculate how much fuel is used based on the fuel points system. The total tank capacity is divided by the full points to get the fuel volume per point.

Next, the fuel added is converted into fuel points by dividing the fuel filled by the liters per point. Then, the actual fuel consumed is determined by subtracting the remaining points from the fuel-filled points.

Finally, mileage is calculated by dividing the total distance traveled by the fuel consumed in points. This gives the fuel efficiency in terms of kilometers per fuel point.

Formula:

Liters per Point = Tank Capacity / Full Points

Fuel Filled in Points = Fuel Filled / Liters per Point

Fuel Consumed in Points = Fuel Filled in Points - Remaining Points

Mileage = Distance Traveled / Fuel Consumed in Points
